</div><p class="copy--feature"><span class="feature">Whether you’re looking for the royal treatment or a quick rub-down, Bali's spa options are seemingly infinite. Indulgent travellers could easily craft a full itinerary around day spas, and find themselves floating from oiled-up acupressure massage to flower petal baths and mani-pedis.</span></p><p>If this sounds like bliss, check out <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bali" class="copy--body__link">Bali</a>'s&nbsp;upmarket hotels first, almost all of which open their doors to the public for spa treatments. Plan ahead, as many of the best day spas are booked well in advance. Don’t forget to dabble in traditional Balinese treatments – they rarely disappoint. With spas in some 70 global locations including three in Bali, it seems the world can’t get enough of the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/kuta-legian/activities/other/mandara-spa" class="copy--body__link">Mandara</a>. </div><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Jamu spa at Alam KulKul Boutique Resort</strong></p><p>Named after a traditional Indonesian medicinal drink, Jamu carries out its treatments using locally sourced products made of herbs, fruits, flowers, roots, spices, honey and essential oils - all enjoyed over the pretty garden courtyard of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/kuta-legian/hotels/alam-kulkul-boutique-resort" class="copy--body__link">AlamKulKul Boutique Resort</a> with a manageable price tag to boot.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">The Spa at The Legian Bali</strong></p><p>Have your body primed, polished and pampered in a tranquil spot&nbsp;overlooking the Indian Ocean. Make use of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/seminyak/hotels/the-legian-bali" class="copy--body__link">The Legian</a>'s hotel setting&nbsp;by dropping into the fitness studio, sauna, steam room or a yoga class.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Shine Spa for Sheraton</strong></p><p>The Sheraton name is synonymous with trusted decadence, and the Shine Spa for <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/kuta-legian/hotels/sheraton-bali-kuta-resort" class="copy--body__link">Sheraton</a>&nbsp;is no exception. But this won’t diminish your ability to unwind: infrared saunas, hair colouring, seaweed facials, 'Mandi Lulur' (an ancient purifying ritual) exfoliating massages and plenty more treatments unfold&nbsp;in <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/seminyak/activities/health-wellness/bodyworks" class="copy--body__link">Bodyworks</a>' grand surrounds.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Spring</strong></p><p>Weary&nbsp;of slow-paced island life? Your salvation is Spring (<a href="http://www.springspa.com/" class="copy--body__link">springspa.com</a>), a self-proclaimed 'social spa' complete with a DJ station, iPads&nbsp;and a loft-style aesthetic. It comes as no surprise that Spring is the brainchild of Ina Bajaj, founder of the well-known East Day Spa and Derek Lockwood, worldwide director of design for Saatchi &amp; Saatchi.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Acqua Perla Spa at the Double-Six Hotel</strong></p><p>Futuristic and fanciful, booking in at the Acqua Perla Spa at the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/kuta-legian/hotels/double-six-luxury-hotel" class="copy--body__link">Double-Six Hotel</a>&nbsp;is – as its website says – 'like accepting a VIP invitation to Alice in Wonderland'. Blue hues and clean glass artworks complement their vast menu.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2015/04/Gold-Eye-treatment-at-Gold-Dust.-Image-by-Samantha-Chalker-Lonely-Planet.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/19615&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2015/04/Gold-Eye-treatment-at-Gold-Dust.-Image-by-Samantha-Chalker-Lonely-Planet.jpg&amp;description=All%20that%20glitters:%20feeling%20rejuvenated%20by%20a%20gold%20eye%20treatment%20at%20Gold%20Dust%20spa,%20Bali.%20Image%20by%20Samantha%20Chalker%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-86966 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2015/04/Gold-Eye-treatment-at-Gold-Dust.-Image-by-Samantha-Chalker-Lonely-Planet.jpg" alt="All that glitters: feeling rejuvenated by a gold eye treatment at Gold Dust spa, Bali. Image by Samantha Chalker / Lonely Planet</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Kerokoban and Canggu</h3><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Gold Dust</strong></p><p>This all-yellow, totally mellow salon is renowned for its world-class facial treatments. Being covered in leaves of gold may sound overly indulgent, but proponents claim it encourages&nbsp;a youthful glow. Gold Dust (<a href="http://www.goldustbali.com/" class="copy--body__link">goldustbali.com</a>) is one of the only great spa haunts on the beach end of Batu Bolong; team your treatment&nbsp;with a nourishing meal from <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/canggu/restaurants/cafe/betelnut-cafe" class="copy--body__link">Betelnut</a>.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Body Temple at the Canggu Club</strong></p><p>If you like to feel a bit of pride and prestige while you’re being pampered, Body Temple at the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/canggu/activities/health-wellness/canggu-club" class="copy--body__link">Canggu Club</a>&nbsp;has your name written all over it. Slimming rituals designed to tackle cellulite and fluid retention, and facials that aim to reduce loss of skin elasticity and pigmentation are some of the menu highlights. Once you’re hooked on Body Temple in <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/canggu" class="copy--body__link">Canggu</a>,&nbsp;check out the second location at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/ungasan-around/hotels/semara-luxury-villa-resort-uluwatu" class="copy--body__link">Semara Luxury Villa Resort</a>&nbsp;in <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/ulu-watu-around" class="copy--body__link">Uluwatu</a>.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Aliyah</strong></p><p>The offspring of the gorgeous Aliyah (<a href="http://www.aliyabali.com/" class="copy--body__link">aliyabali.com</a>) bohemian bungalows, this is a cheap and cheerful set-up found on Jl Umalas I. Team your treatment with a colourful, preservative-free meal at the homestay’s restaurant, Sumac, for an&nbsp;afternoon of heart-warming bliss.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2015/04/The-soothing-setting-of-Kush.-Image-by-Samantha-Chalker-Lonely-Planet.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/19615&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2015/04/The-soothing-setting-of-Kush.-Image-by-Samantha-Chalker-Lonely-Planet.jpg&amp;description=Getting%20centred%20in%20the%20soothing%20setting%20of%20Kush%20spa%20in%20Ubud,%20Bali.%20Image%20by%20Samantha%20Chalker%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-86967 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2015/04/The-soothing-setting-of-Kush.-Image-by-Samantha-Chalker-Lonely-Planet.jpg" alt="Getting centred in the soothing setting of Kush spa in Ubud, Bali. Image by Samantha Chalker / Lonely Planet"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Getting centred in the soothing setting of Kush spa in Ubud, Bali. Image by Samantha Chalker / Lonely Planet</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Ubud</h3><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Kush Ayurvedic Rejuvenation</strong></p><p>Created&nbsp;by the gurus behind <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bali/ubud/activities/health-wellness/yoga-barn" class="copy--body__link">Yoga Barn</a>, Kush's yogic roots are clear. The&nbsp;walls are covered with Sanskrit affirmations and massages include pranayama breathing instructions. One session may start an addiction and see you checking in to the Barn’s guesthouse for a full rejuvenation retreat.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Bodyworks Centre</strong></p><p>Not to be confused with the Seminyak spa of the same name, the Bodyworks Centre (<a href="http://ubudbodyworkscentre.com/" class="copy--body__link">ubudbodyworkscentre.com</a>) of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bali/ubud" class="copy--body__link">Ubud</a>&nbsp;is the sacred space of famed Balinese healer, Ketut Arsana. This is not your traditional spa session: it’s a chance for deep individualised healing, which may include energy balancing, cupping, reflexology, therapeutic massage, chakra healing and more – all depending on your body’s requirements. An authentic experience to say the least.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Ubud Sari Health Resort</strong></p><p>Enjoy a 'total tissue cleansing' with organic and natural ingredients at Ubud Sari Health Resort (<a href="http://www.ubudsari.com/" class="copy--body__link">ubudsari.com</a>).&nbsp;Function definitely trumps form at this no-nonsense spa, salon and hotel, with a copious range of offerings.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Spa Bali</strong></p><p>The newest of Spa Bali's (<a href="http://spabali.biz/" class="copy--body__link">spabali.biz</a>) four island locations offers chocolate body scrubs, human hair extensions and Ayurvedic massages. Stroll in at 10am and leave when the doors close an hour before midnight.</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Bukit Peninsula, Jimbaran and Nusa Dua</h3><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Jari Menari</strong></p><p>Jari Menari (<a href="http://www.jarimenarinusadua.com/" class="copy--body__link">jarimenarinusadua.com</a>) might be known and adored for its ability to soothe&nbsp;clients into peaceful oblivion, but its good deeds earn the gold star. The establishment's entire premise is to give work to young local males with few skills and opportunities. Learn the Jari Menari art of 'dancing fingers' at a one-day workshop and book any visit well in advance: customers roll in as quickly as their awards. Swing between their centres in both <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/nusa-dua" class="copy--body__link">Nusa Dua</a>&nbsp;and Seminyak.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Spa on the Rocks, Ayana Spa</strong></p><p>Its location, perched on a rock in the Indian Ocean, is just part of the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/around-jimbaran/hotels/ayana-resort-and-spa-bali" class="copy--body__link">Ayana Resort and Spa</a>'s&nbsp;appeal – though admittedly a very big part. The treatment catalogue is laced with black rice baths, volcanic lava body scrubs, jade stone ceremonies, diamonds, pearls and champagne. This is quite possibly Bali’s most indulgent spa splurge.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Temple Spa at The Temple Lodge</strong></p><p>What could be more reassuring than a massage at the hands of a man known as Mr Tendon? The sanctuary of the spa at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bingin/hotels/temple-lodge" class="copy--body__link">The Temple Lodge</a>&nbsp;is the perfect drop-in for stiff surfers on the way home from <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bingin" class="copy--body__link">Bingin</a>&nbsp;beach.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Thalasso Bali at Grand Mirage Resort</strong></p><p>There are many incredible resort spas in Nusa Dua, but Thalasso Bali at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/tanjung-benoa/hotels/grand-mirage-resort-thalasso-bali" class="copy--body__link">Grand Mirage</a>&nbsp;sets itself apart with a 38-degree, 100% seaweed and seawater aqua medic pool, as well as a cave-like treatment room for shower massages.</p><p These low-key hangouts make every day an indulgence.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Air Panas Banjar</strong></p><p>At <a href="%20http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/west-bali/activities/other/air-panas-banjar%20" class="copy--body__link">Air Panas Banjar</a>, hot springs percolate down through a series of pools amid lush tropical plants. Soak in the top pools, or get a pummelling massage from the falling water.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Lovina</strong></p><p>Relaxed, low-key, low-rise... <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bali/lovina" class="copy--body__link">Lovina</a> is <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/kuta-legian" class="copy--body__link">Kuta</a>'s&nbsp;polar opposite. Even the waves are calm. Watch dolphins bound out of the ocean and fishing boats drift along the shore.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Amed</strong></p><p>Time stands still in the town of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bali/amed-and-the-far-east-coast" class="copy--body__link">Amed</a>&nbsp;and the cute café scene tops the vibe right off. Hit the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/indonesia/bali/amed-and-the-far-east-coast/hotels/aiona-garden-of-health" class="copy--body__link">Aiona Garden of Health</a>&nbsp;for a kombucha tea and some homemade mango jams.</p><p><em>This article was first published by Lonely Planet in November 2009, and was updated by Samantha Chalker in April 2015.&nbsp;</em></p></div>
