</div><p class="copy--feature"><span class="feature">Home to a galaxy of movie stars, many of the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa" class="copy--body__link">USA</a>’s most famously wealthy zip codes and over 300 days of sunshine every year, <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/los-angeles-san-diego-southern-california" class="copy--body__link">Southern California</a> is the perfect place to satisfy your craving for a little – or maybe a lot of – luxury. Whether you aim to hit the beaches or unwind at a sybaritic spa, we’ve got you covered. Here are our top picks for indulgent trips all around SoCal.</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">1. Spa time in Palm Springs</h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/southern-californias-top-luxury-experiences&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0002_A3552683489_06b8ab296e_o.jpg&amp;description=Colony%20Palms%20Hotel,%20Palm%20Springs.%20Image%20by%20Soraya%20S.%20/%20CC%20BY%202.0" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="aligncenter wp-image-75187 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0002_A3552683489_06b8ab296e_o.jpg" alt="Colony Palms Hotel, Palm Springs. Image by Soraya S. / CC BY 2.0"><span class="copy--caption img--caption">Colony Palms Hotel, Palm Springs. <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/sorayas/3552683489" class="copy--body__link">Image by Soraya S.</a> / <a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/" class="copy--body__link">CC BY 2.0</a></span></p><p>In <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/hollywood" class="copy--body__link">Hollywood’s</a> early days, the desert town of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/california-deserts" class="copy--body__link">Palm Springs</a> was as far from <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles" class="copy--body__link">LA</a> as movie stars were legally allowed to travel by their studio contracts. A resort playground soon sprang up in the middle of the Mojave, eventually attracting a who’s-who list of everyone from Frank Sinatra to Elvis. Now hip again, ‘PS’ has made a comeback by catering to city-weary souls looking for a fabulous weekend of pampering. Check into the 1960s-chic <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/palm-springs-and-coachella-valley/hotels/viceroy-palm-springs" class="copy--body__link">Viceroy Palm Springs</a>, then lounge in a poolside cabana with cocktails after a deep tissue massage, oxygen facial or salt scrub at the resort’s stylish Estrella Spa. Or get manicured alongside celebs and society ladies at the ritzy, glitzy Palm Springs Yacht Club at the posh <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/palm-springs-and-coachella-valley/hotels/parker-palm-springs" class="copy--body__link">Parker Palm Springs</a>.</p><h3 class="copy--h3">2. Star spotting in Los Angeles</h3><p>You could rub shoulders with celebrities almost anywhere in LA, even standing in line at a coffee shop. Of course, your odds of spotting a real-life movie star are much better if you imitate the lifestyles of the rich and famous. On the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/sights/other/sunset-strip" class="copy--body__link">Sunset Strip</a>, book a garden cottage at the vaunted <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/hotels/chateau-marmont" class="copy--body__link">Chateau Marmont</a>, where celebs pad barefooted around the lobby and sip martinis at the bar. Strut like a supermodel into <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/shopping/fashion-accessories/fred-segal-3-1038483" class="copy--body__link">Fred Segal</a>, an ivy-covered boutique on <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/sights/neighbourhoods-villages/melrose-ave" class="copy--body__link">Melrose Ave</a> that’s an A-list must-have. Hollywood’s velvet-roped nightclubs and bars offer some wild nights out, but star sightings are a lot cheaper – not to mention more up close and personal – at deluxe <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/entertainment-nightlife/other/arclight-cinemas" class="copy--body__link">ArcLight Cinemas</a> inside the landmark 1960s-era geodesic Cinerama Dome.</p><div class="halfway-links--related media">

</div><h3 class="copy--h3">3. Cruise in a convertible to Malibu</h3><p>Make this one California dream come true: rent a Tesla or Ferrari California and enjoy a sun-kissed road trip along coastal Hwy 1, also known as the Pacific Coast Hwy. From <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/santa-monica" class="copy--body__link">Santa Monica</a>, a classy beach town at the western edge of LA, it’s only a 20-mile drive to Malibu, a wealthy celebrity enclave, where impossibly beautiful beaches are strung like polished pearls along the roadside. Gawk at the pro board riders at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/sights/beaches-islands-waterfronts/surfrider-beach" class="copy--body__link">Surfrider Beach</a> by Malibu’s pier, then slip into your bikini and spread out your beach towel at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/los-angeles/sights/beaches-islands-waterfronts/zuma-westward-beach" class="copy--body__link">Zuma Beach</a>, an irresistible blonde mane of sand farther west.</p><h3 class="copy--h3">4. Hot air balloons &amp; horse racing in Del Mar</h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/southern-californias-top-luxury-experiences&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0003_147182855.jpg&amp;description=Hot%20air%20balloons%20over%20Del%20Mar.%20Image%20by%20Lee%20Sie%20Photography%20/%20Moment%20Open%20/%20Getty%20Images" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="aligncenter wp-image-75188 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0003_147182855.jpg" alt="Hot air balloons over Del Mar. Image by Lee Sie Photography / Moment Open / Getty Images"><span class="copy--caption img--caption">Hot air balloons over Del Mar. Image by Lee Sie Photography / Moment Open / Getty Images</span></p><p>There’s no posher place on <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/san-diego" class="copy--body__link">San Diego’s</a> North County Coast than <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/del-mar" class="copy--body__link">Del Mar</a>. Spend a summer day at the races at Del Mar Thoroughbred Club (www.dmtc.com), once a favourite of Hollywood glitterati and where Seabiscuit ran in 1938. It’s worth getting tickets for opening day just to see the outrageous, over-the-top hats. Del Mar also happens to be the best spot on the SoCal coast for a memorable hot-air balloon ride, complete with a champagne toast; catch a sunset over the Pacific from 1500ft up in the air with California Dreamin’ (www.californiadreamin.com).</p><h3 class="copy--h3">5. Go sailing at Newport Beach</h3><p>Nowhere else do cinematic images of SoCal beaches come to life more vividly than in <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/orange-county-beaches" class="copy--body__link">Orange County</a>, especially at chi-chi <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/newport-beach" class="copy--body__link">Newport Beach</a> (as seen on TV’s The OC and Arrested Development). On the picturesque <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/newport-beach/activities/water-sports/balboa-peninsula" class="copy--body__link">Balboa Peninsula</a>, you can rent the ultimate status symbol: your own boat. Don’t worry, you won’t need any nautical experience to be your own captain with Duffy Electric Boat Rentals (www.duffyofnewportbeach.com). All you need is a bunch of friends and some music for a fun evening floating around Newport Harbor. When you get hungry, tie up at a waterfront restaurant and have dinner delivered on board.</p><h3 class="copy--h3">6. Beach resort life in Coronado</h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/southern-californias-top-luxury-experiences&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0001_C5431670001_68a204c835_o.jpg&amp;description=Vista%20Walk%20at%20the%20Hotel%20del%20Coronado.%20Image%20by%20hoteldelcoronado%20/%20CC%20BY%202.0" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="aligncenter wp-image-75186 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0001_C5431670001_68a204c835_o.jpg" alt="Vista Walk at the Hotel del Coronado. Image by hoteldelcoronado / CC BY 2.0"><span class="copy--caption img--caption">Vista Walk at the Hotel del Coronado. <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/29541450@N07/5431670001" class="copy--body__link">Image by hoteldelcoronado</a> / <a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/" class="copy--body__link">CC BY 2.0</a></span></p><p>Across the bay from downtown San Diego, peninsular Coronado is a trip back in time. Pretend you’ve joined the ranks of yesteryear’s social elites at the historic <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/san-diego/hotels/hotel-del-coronado" class="copy--body__link">Hotel Del Coronado</a>, featured in the movie Some Like It Hot starring Marilyn Monroe. Dating from the late Victorian era, the red-turreted ‘Hotel Del’ is a genteel seaside resort so all-encompassing that you’ll rarely be tempted to leave the premises. Set out a chair on the beach, cocoon yourself inside the tip-top spa, clink cocktail glasses at the Babcock &amp; Story Bar and feast on fresh seafood at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/san-diego/restaurants/american/1500-ocean" class="copy--body__link">1500 Ocean</a> restaurant. All that aside, one good reason to venture outside the resort is to cycle down Coronado’s famous Silver Strand, a collection of idyllic white-sand public beaches.</p><h3 class="copy--h3">7. Escape to Catalina Island</h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/southern-californias-top-luxury-experiences&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0000_D5347037376_4545e3f490_o.jpg&amp;description=Avalon%20harbour,%20Catalina%20Island.%20Image%20by%20Justin%20Ennis%20/%20CC%20BY%202.0" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="aligncenter wp-image-75185 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2014/07/Ss_0000_D5347037376_4545e3f490_o.jpg" alt="Avalon harbour, Catalina Island. Image by Justin Ennis / CC BY 2.0"><span class="copy--caption img--caption">Avalon harbour, Catalina Island. <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/averain/5347037376" class="copy--body__link">Image by Justin Ennis</a> / <a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/" class="copy--body__link">CC BY 2.0</a></span></p><p>Only an hour’s sail from LA or Orange County, <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/california/catalina-island" class="copy--body__link">Catalina Island</a> is the most civilized of SoCal’s jewel-like Channel Islands. Once owned by chewing-gum magnate William Wrigley Jr, this Mediterranean-esque island is now largely a nature preserve, which you can explore via the ‘Safari Bus.’ Ferries dock in the small harbour town of Avalon, where couples dine on fresh-caught seafood at romantic bistros and tryst inside breezy boutique hotels. During the day, tour Catalina’s 1920s art-deco casino, prettily adorned with murals, or the botanical gardens where rare plants grow.</p></div>
