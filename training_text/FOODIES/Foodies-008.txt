class="copy--h3">How to start&nbsp;a super(food) day</h3><p>It's tradition in <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru/lima" class="copy--body__link">Lima</a> to start the morning (or night) off with piping hot cups&nbsp;of herb-infused drinks that are&nbsp;boiled with grains like quinoa or boosted by <em>maca</em>, a&nbsp;revitalizing&nbsp;root from the high Andes of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru" class="copy--body__link">Peru</a>. Find the carts set up at bustling intersections from the break of dawn until stock runs out, and back again when the sun sets. Don't forget to ask for the <em>yapa</em>, the leftover pour that couldn't fit in the first serving. </h3><p>When life gives you 4000 varieties of potatoes, get creative. The <em>papa rellena</em> is Peru’s equivalent to a twice-baked potato. Completely enclosed and thus easy to eat on the go, mashed potato is stuffed with seasoned ground beef, onions, olives and egg before being fried to golden perfection. You’ll find plenty of options within the first three blocks of Av Petit Thouars, near central Lima, where students of nearby universities scramble to get the best and tastiest deal. Top it off with ketchup or Peru's&nbsp;ever-present spicy chili sauce, <em>aji</em>.</p><div class="halfway-links--related media">

In the case of Peru's giant-kernel corn, that 'something' is anise. The small, aromatic seed gives the boiled corn a hint of sweetness that, when paired with a thick slab of <em>queso Andino</em> (a typical salty cheese in Peru), bursts into big flavor. Carts neighbor Lima’s <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru/lima/sights/museums-galleries/museo-de-arte-de-lima" class="copy--body__link">Museum of Art</a> during lunch hours,&nbsp;but&nbsp;at night, find the tastiest on&nbsp;Angamos at&nbsp;Jr Dante in Surquillo.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/4-huevo-de-codorniz-30d77ec45ea9.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/4-huevo-de-codorniz-30d77ec45ea9.jpg&amp;description=These%20small%20eggs%20pack%20a%20protein%20punch%20and%20have%20a%20soft,%20creamy%20texture%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107868 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/4-huevo-de-codorniz-30d77ec45ea9.jpg" alt="These small eggs pack a protein punch and have a soft, creamy texture © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> These small eggs pack a protein punch and have a soft, creamy texture © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Quail-ity snack</h3><p>Although <em>pollerias</em> (roast chicken restaurants) appear to be on every corner in Lima, <em>huevos de codorniz</em> (quail eggs) rule the roost when it comes to street food. Vendors with small push carts first hard boil the eggs then, if&nbsp;you prefer, peel the spotted shells to reveal the creamy insides which are then generously sprinkled with salt. Nearly half a dozen of these small eggs&nbsp;can be purchased for one sol, and are found outside of shopping center <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru/lima/shopping/markets-streets-arcades/polvos-azules" class="copy--body__link">Polvos Azules</a> in central Lima or any district market.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/5-picarones4-a0c34927cbed.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/5-picarones4-a0c34927cbed.jpg&amp;description=Vegetable%20donuts?%20Sort%20of.%20Picarones%20are%20hot%20and%20ready%20in%20Parque%20Kennedy%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-107869 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/5-picarones4-a0c34927cbed.jpg" alt="Vegetable donuts? Sort of. Picarones are hot and ready in Parque Kennedy © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> Vegetable donuts? Sort of. Picarones are hot and ready in Parque Kennedy © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">To do or donut?</h3><p>In Peru, such hole-y goodness as the donut and other fried pastries are upped a level&nbsp;by incorporating native starchy vegetables. <em>Picarones</em> opt for sweet potato and squash, hence their orange hue. Rings of this naturally sweet batter are lightly fried before being bathed&nbsp;in a generous pour of Peruvian honey. Their pastry cousins, <em>yuquitas</em>, use flour from the yucca root to become a warm, air-puffed treat. Get the best bang for your buck (or sol) at Mercado Palermo in La Victoria for <em>yuquitas</em>, and Parque Kennedy in Miraflores for <em>picarones</em>.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/6-arroz-48570819dd3e.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/6-arroz-48570819dd3e.jpg&amp;description=There%20are%20many%20variations%20on%20the%20classic%20Peruvian%20rice%20pudding%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107870 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/6-arroz-48570819dd3e.jpg" alt="There are many variations on the classic Peruvian rice pudding © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> There are many variations on the classic Peruvian rice pudding © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">The dark side of rice pudding</h3><p>There are various ways to jazz&nbsp;up the classic dessert <em>arroz con leche</em> (rice pudding), such as the <em>combinado,</em> which fills half of the serving bowl with <em>mazamorra</em>, a full bodied, jam-like pudding made from purple corn and native fruits. Oddly enough, this thick sauce tastes like blackberries. Get nutty and order up the <em>arroz zambito</em>, rice pudding sweetened with Peruvian <em>chancaca</em>&nbsp;sauce (an unrefined sugar syrup infused with orange peel) and topped with pecans and raisins. Treat yourself just outside of Real Plaza on Av Wilson in central Lima.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/7-anticuchos-190a50396529.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/7-anticuchos-190a50396529.jpg&amp;description=It%20may%20sound%20morbid,%20but%20these%20smokey%20hearts%20are%20readily%20available%20in%20Lima%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107871 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/7-anticuchos-190a50396529.jpg" alt="It may sound morbid, but these smokey hearts are readily available in Lima © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> These smoky hearts are readily available in Lima © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Everything's better on a stick...</h3><p>...Even cow heart. If the thought of eating a cow's heart makes you uneasy, don't think you're the first. However if you can resist the smell of the grilled, tender cutlets on a cool evening, served kebab-style alongside boiled potato and a dab (or smothering) of hot sauce, then you've got incredible willpower. These smoky cuts of meat, known as&nbsp;<em>anticuchos</em>, are a perfect example of how innovative and waste-free traditional Peruvian cooking can be. Look for these when the sun goes down, along Av Angamos in Surquillo or Av La Mar in Miraflores.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/8-everything-cart-e4ef928e4d75.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/8-everything-cart-e4ef928e4d75.jpg&amp;description=For%20any%20craving,%20sweet%20or%20salty,%20these%20bike%20carts%20have%20the%20answer%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107872 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/8-everything-cart-e4ef928e4d75.jpg" alt="For any craving, sweet or salty, these bike carts have the answer © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> For any craving, sweet or salty, these bike carts have the answer © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Cheap thrills</h3><p>They say if you want adventure in Peru, you can best find it in the mountains of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru/huaraz-and-the-cordilleras/huaraz" class="copy--body__link">Huaraz</a>&nbsp;or in&nbsp;<a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru/arequipa-and-canyon-country/canon-del-colca" class="copy--body__link">Colca Canyon</a>. But the real adrenaline-seekers are the bikers who pedal carts full of palm-sized pizzas, empanadas, slices of apple pie and more along Lima’s traffic-ridden streets. Stocked with something for every craving, keep an eye out for these carts along Av Arequipa, which runs from Miraflores to central Lima. We recommend the baked chicken empanadas with a squeeze of lemon and drizzling of <em>aji</em> sauce.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/9-agua-de-coco3-2e49d82cb1c2.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/9-agua-de-coco3-2e49d82cb1c2.jpg&amp;description=Munch%20on%20cocount%20snacks%20in%20the%20bohemian%20district%20of%20Barranco%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107873 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/9-agua-de-coco3-2e49d82cb1c2.jpg" alt="Munch on cocount snacks in the bohemian district of Barranco © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> Munch on coconut snacks in the bohemian district of Barranco © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Cuckoo for coco</h3><p>Renowned for its rich biodiversity, Lima is the melting pot of Peru’s edible treasures. Coconuts from the beachy terrains up north are transformed into about as many different treats as you can count on your sticky, sweet fingers. Giant coconut macaroons known as <em>cocadas</em>, available in white or brown sugar options, will take you to a tropics state of mind. Stay hydrated with pure coconut water, perfect for the city’s notoriously muggy weather. Look for a parked cart in the trendy <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/peru/lima/barranco/things-to-do" class="copy--body__link">Barranco</a> district on Jr Sucre near Plaza Chabuca Granda.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/10-ceviche-490eec8dcccb.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/peruvian-street-eats-limas-top-10-meals-on-wheels&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/10-ceviche-490eec8dcccb.jpg&amp;description=Ceviche%20is%20served%20from%20street%20corner%20carts%20in%20Lima%20%C2%A9%20Agnes%20Rivera%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107874 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/10-ceviche-490eec8dcccb.jpg" alt="Ceviche is served from street corner carts in Lima © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet"> Ceviche is served from street corner carts in Lima © Agnes Rivera / Lonely Planet</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Ceviche al paso</h3><p>As a coastal town, it’s not hard to find a ceviche restaurant in Lima with a view of the ocean. While day-to-day life doesn’t always allow for such luxury, a pang for seafood marinated in lime juice can hit at any moment, so vendors have brought it to the streets. Some <em>ceviche al paso</em> carts are so popular that you’ll still have to wait in line, but that’s a good sign. In the case of raw fish, it’s best to go with a recommendation, and ours are those just outside of district markets such as stands near Surquillo’s Mercado #1 or #2.</p></div>
