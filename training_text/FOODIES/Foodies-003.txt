</div><p class="copy--feature"><span class="feature">While Dubai has plenty of intriguing cuisines on offer, from Pakistani and Peruvian to Ethiopian and Iranian, it's always been a little trickier&nbsp;to find authentic UAE fare in the city. Traditionally, these dishes have just been served in homes or at major celebrations, but that's finally starting to change as a&nbsp;</span><span class="feature">decent selection of Emirati restaurants&nbsp;crop up across town.</span></p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/SMCCU_cultural_meal-bc516818f47b-1.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/taste-of-dubai-the-citys-best-emirati-eats&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/SMCCU_cultural_meal-bc516818f47b-1.jpg&amp;description=Food%20is%20served%20in%20the%20majlis%20at%20the%20Sheikh%20Mohammed%20Centre%20for%20Cultural%20Understanding%20%C2%A9%20Glen%20Pearson" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-108160 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/SMCCU_cultural_meal-bc516818f47b-1.jpg" alt="Food is served in the majlis at the Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding © Glen Pearson"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Food is served in the majlis at the Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding © Glen Pearson</span></p><p>So what exactly is Emirati cuisine? Hearty meat dishes born in the desert and seafood from the Arabian Gulf, usually served with flatbread and rice. Bezar, a blend of roasted and ground spices including coriander, cumin, turmeric and cinnamon, is added to practically everything, while centuries-old trading partners such as Iran and India have also left their mark on the cuisine. Many of the newer restaurants aren't just sticking to a traditional menu though: camel sliders and chicken tikka-stuffed breads are just a couple of the unexpected Emirati-fusion treats on offer. Here’s where to get your fill.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Contemporary cooking at Aseelah</strong></p><p>With dishes like date-stuffed chicken roulade and juicy camel sliders, Aseelah at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/hotels/radisson-blu-hotel-dubai-deira-creek" class="copy--body__link">Radisson Blu Hotel, Dubai Deira Creek</a> serves up the city’s most adventurous and accomplished take on local cuisine. Old-school favourites are not forgotten; chef Uwe Micheel has spent years visiting Emirati families to master recipes like prawns marinated in bezar and <em>aseeda bobar</em> (pumpkin pudding). This stylish spot is the only Emirati restaurant that serves booze, with creative cocktails and a well-priced wine list.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Aseelah_Camel_sliders-cdc922709763.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/taste-of-dubai-the-citys-best-emirati-eats&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Aseelah_Camel_sliders-cdc922709763.jpg&amp;description=A%20trio%20of%20camel%20sliders%20with%20different%20toppings%20including%20date%20and%20lime%20jam%20%C2%A9%20NikAndTam" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-108156 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Aseelah_Camel_sliders-cdc922709763.jpg" alt="A trio of camel sliders with different toppings including date and lime jam © NikAndTam"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">A trio of camel sliders with different toppings including date and lime jam © NikAndTam</span></p><div class="halfway-links--related media">

</div><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Authentic flavours at Al Fanar</strong></p><p>Al Fanar is a kitsch, fun&nbsp;spot, with food and décor harking back to the pre-oil days. Don’t let the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/shopping/shopping-centre/dubai-festival-city-mall" class="copy--body__link">Festival City Mall</a> location put you off; designed like an old courtyard house, the restaurant is hugely atmospheric (just ignore the dodgy waxworks). First-timers are encouraged to try chicken <em>machboos</em> (a bezar-spiced rice dish) and tender <em>naghar mashwi</em>&nbsp;(grilled squid). There’s a second branch at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/restaurants/other/al-fanar" class="copy--body__link">Town Centre Jumeriah</a>.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Home-style cooking at Al Tawasol</strong></p><p>Locals have been flocking to the family-run <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/restaurants/middle-eastern/al-tawasol" class="copy--body__link">Al Tawasol</a> in <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/deira" class="copy--body__link">Deira</a> for food-like-Grandma-used-to-make since 1999. Take a seat on a&nbsp;corner of carpet in the main dining area or in one of the private tented majlis, then scoop up succulent lamb <em>machboos</em> and spicy <em>salona</em> (curry) with your hands. Al Tawasol also does a mean <em>mandi</em>, a Yemeni dish that’s been adopted across the Arabian Peninsula: meat slow-cooked in a tandoor and served over aromatic rice.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Al_Tawasol-1395e1a65dab.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/taste-of-dubai-the-citys-best-emirati-eats&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Al_Tawasol-1395e1a65dab.jpg&amp;description=A%20platter%20of%20lamb%20salona%20and%20chicken%20mandi%20at%20Al%20Tawasol%20%C2%A9%20Glen%20Pearson" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-108155 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Al_Tawasol-1395e1a65dab.jpg" alt="A platter of lamb salona and chicken mandi at Al Tawasol © Glen Pearson"> A platter of lamb salona and chicken mandi at Al Tawasol © Glen Pearson</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Camel milk treats at The Majlis</strong></p><p>With intricate mashrabiya and a blue-tiled fountain, The Majlis (<a href="http://themajlisdubai.com/" class="copy--body__link">themajlisdubai.com</a>) at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/shopping/shopping-centre/dubai-mall" class="copy--body__link">Dubai Mall</a> specialises in coffee, cakes, shakes and ice cream made from camel milk. A staple of the Bedouin diet until the mid-20th century, it’s lower in fat, and higher in vitamins and minerals, than the cow equivalent. Try a camelccino made with the café’s own blend of Ethiopian beans, paired with a pistachio-glazed éclair made with – you guessed it – camel milk.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Trendy-meets-traditional at Seven Sands</strong></p><p>Spread over two floors at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/sights/beaches-islands-waterfronts/beach-jbr" class="copy--body__link">The Beach&nbsp;at JBR</a>, Seven Sands (<a href="http://sevensandsrestaurant.com/en/pages/default" class="copy--body__link">sevensandsrestaurant.com</a>) features sleek Arabesque interiors and a breezy terrace overlooking the sea. Blending traditional with trendy, the menu is full of Emirati classics, but you’ll also see dishes from the wider region such as velvety hummus and crumbly kibbeh. Dishes to try? Sambousas – similar to Indian&nbsp;samosas but given a bezar spice twist – and prawn <em>fouga</em> flavoured with bezar, saffron and dry limes.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Seven_Sands_prawn_fouga-21006b004496.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/taste-of-dubai-the-citys-best-emirati-eats&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Seven_Sands_prawn_fouga-21006b004496.jpg&amp;description=Prawn%20fouga%20(bezar%20spices%20cooked%20together%20with%20perfumed%20stock)%20at%20Seven%20Sands%20%C2%A9%20Glen%20Pearson" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-108438 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Seven_Sands_prawn_fouga-21006b004496.jpg" alt="Prawn fouga (bezar spices cooked together with perfumed stock) at Seven Sands © Glen Pearson"> Prawn fouga (bezar spices cooked together with perfumed stock) at Seven Sands © Glen Pearson</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Creative khameer at Logma</strong></p><p>Located in trendy <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/shopping/shopping-centre/boxpark" class="copy--body__link">BoxPark</a>, Logma (<a href="http://logma.ae/" class="copy--body__link">logma.ae</a>) is a&nbsp;hip eatery with modern interiors – think funky camel motifs and hanging kerosene lamps – and casual, contemporary Emirati fare. &nbsp;It’s a top spot for lunch with soft <em>khameer</em> flatbread&nbsp;stuffed with fillings such as chicken tikka, or smothered in more traditional cream cheese and dibs. Order with a side of Logma’s famous fries seasoned with Khaleeji spices.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Cultural meals at Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding</strong></p><p>For a crash course in both Emirati cuisine and culture, visit the <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/sights/arts-culture-literary/sheikh-mohammed-centre-cultural-understanding" class="copy--body__link">Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding</a> in a renovated wind-tower house in the historic <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/sights/neighbourhoods-villages/al-fahidi-historic-district" class="copy--body__link">Al Fahidi</a> district. Dishes such as chicken <em>machboos</em> and lip-smackingly sweet <em>luqaimat</em> (donuts) drizzled in <em>dibs&nbsp;</em>(date syrup) are served while sitting cross-legged on carpets and cushions on the floor. Hosted by young Emirati volunteers, visitors are encouraged to ask questions about local culture, with no topic off-limits.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/SMCCU_majlis_with_presenter-a98716ecd636.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/taste-of-dubai-the-citys-best-emirati-eats&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/SMCCU_majlis_with_presenter-a98716ecd636.jpg&amp;description=A%20cultural%20meal%20at%20the%20Sheikh%20Mohammed%20Centre%20for%20Cultural%20Understanding%20%C2%A9%20Glen%20Pearson" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-108161 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/SMCCU_majlis_with_presenter-a98716ecd636.jpg" alt="A cultural meal at the Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding © Glen Pearson"> A cultural meal at the Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding © Glen Pearson</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Comfort food at Al Barza</strong></p><p>Below a kandora shop on Jumeirah Beach Road, a block back from the beach and 500 metres north of Mercato Mall, Al Barza features sandy interiors with dark latticework and an outdoor terrace. Open for breakfast, lunch and dinner, feel-good favourites include <em>balaleet</em> (scrambled eggs with cardamom-scented vermicelli noodles) and creamy <em>harees</em> (wheat porridge with shredded lamb). More adventurous diners can try <em>tahtah malleh</em>, a salt-cured fish and rice dish that is something of an acquired taste.</p><p><strong class="copy--body__strong">Casual bites at Milas </strong></p><p>Popular with Emiratis, Milas (<a href="http://milas.cc" class="copy--body__link">milas.cc</a>) is a buzzing spot at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/united-arab-emirates/dubai/shopping/shopping-centre/dubai-mall" class="copy--body__link">Dubai Mall</a> that combines glossy black and warm wood interiors with accessible Emirati cuisine. Munch on cumin-sprinkled&nbsp;<em>dangaw</em>&nbsp;(boiled chickpeas) and still-steaming khameer while you peruse the large menu presented on an iPad. Start with endless cups of&nbsp;Arabic coffee&nbsp;brewed with cardamom served with sticky dates, followed by the signature <em>mbahar deyay</em> (chicken in a spicy cream sauce with saffron-infused rice).</p></div>
