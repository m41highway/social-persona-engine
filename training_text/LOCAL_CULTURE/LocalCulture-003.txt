Hiking is a popular activity among Hong Kong residents and tourists."> Hit the MacLehose Trail in Hong Kong © Kay Dulay / Getty Images</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Dust off your trail runners in Hong Kong</h3><p>The summer rains have gone, and the mercury has dropped (slightly), making November the perfect time to strap on your hiking boots and hit the trail. Mountain tops barely peek above <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/china/hong-kong" class="copy--body__link">Hong Kong</a>’s iconic skyscraper-studded skyline, hinting at the hundreds of kilometres of wild trails waiting to be explored just beyond the hum of the city.</p><p>The gutsy can enter the Oxfam Trailwalker (<a href="http://www.oxfamtrailwalker.org.hk/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">oxfamtrailwalker.org.hk</a>; 18-20 November). This annual 48-hour endurance challenge covers the MacLehose Trail, a 100km undulating strip that tiptoes along the ridges of the New Territories and descends down to some of Hong Kong’s best beaches, as well as the unusual rock formations of the Unesco-listed <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/china/hong-kong/sights/parks-gardens/hong-kong-global-geopark" class="copy--body__link">Global Geopark</a>.</p><div class="halfway-links--related media">

sip a perfect macchiato in <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/ethiopia/northern-ethiopia/gonder" class="copy--body__link">Gonder</a> before wandering through <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/ethiopia/northern-ethiopia/gonder/sights/castles-palaces-mansions/royal-enclosure" class="copy--body__link">16th-century castles and palaces</a>; and descend into a world frozen in stone at Lalibela, the site of 11 spellbinding rock-hewn churches.</p><p>If you’d like to get even closer to the compelling African sky, climb up into <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/ethiopia/northern-ethiopia/simien-mountains-national-park" class="copy--body__link">Simien Mountains National Park</a>. But be warned, you’ll spend most of your time looking down – the views over the Abyssinian abysses are mesmerising, as are the sights of enigmatic bleeding heart monkeys (geladas) and walia ibex.</p><p>The northern town of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/ethiopia/northern-ethiopia/aksum" class="copy--body__link">Aksum</a>, famed for its ancient civilisation, towering <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/ethiopia/northern-ethiopia/aksum/sights/historic/northern-stelae-field" class="copy--body__link">monolithic stelae</a> and its claim to house the Ark of the Covenant, swells at the end of the month with thousands of followers who’ve made the pilgrimage to celebrate the Ethiopian Orthodox festival of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/ethiopia/northern-ethiopia/aksum/events/religious-spiritual/festival-maryam-zion" class="copy--body__link">Maryam Zion</a>. It’s a fascinating glimpse into the devotion (and pomp and circumstance) associated with the religion.</p><p><em>Matt Phillips – Destination Editor, sub-Saharan Africa. Strombus gigas is included in Appendix II of the UNEP's CITES list of endangered species"> Celebrate all things sea snail in Turks and Caicos © Travel Ink / Getty Images</p><h3 class="copy--h3">Test your taste buds in Turks &amp; Caicos</h3><p>In November, most of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/caribbean" class="copy--body__link">the Caribbean</a> is in the last throes of hurricane season, but the rains and storms soon subside, giving way to an ideal shoulder season that allows travellers to avoid the crowds and high prices that arrive in December.</p><p><a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/turks-and-caicos" class="copy--body__link">Turks and Caicos</a> shakes off the weather and jumps right into its own festivities – at the end of the month, the islands hold the world’s only conch festival, a celebration of the delicious gastropod that is a staple of local cuisine. The main event is the ‘Conch-etition’, a tasting competition in which chefs compete for the top prize in several categories including Best Conch Chowder, Best Conch Salad, Best Specialty Conch and Best in Show. Attendees can also show off their own conch-cooking skills in the Home Kitchen competition – whip up your best sea snail recipe and see if it takes home the prize.</p><p>After you’ve eaten your fill, head to one of the islands’ pristine beaches to rest up, or get moving by hiking to historic ruins on North Caicos or trying your hand at kiteboarding the waters off <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/turks-and-caicos/caicos-islands/providenciales" class="copy--body__link">Providenciales</a>.</p><p><em>Bailey Johnson – Destination Editor, Central America &amp; Caribbean. The smell of roasted chestnuts rises up from food carts on every street corner, piercing the cool autumnal air with their pungent sweetness. Fallen leaves crunch underfoot with each step. Fantastical and festive displays fill the massive storefront windows along the city’s shopping corridors. The excitement of the approaching holiday season manifests itself all over the city.</p><p>The holiday markets at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/new-york-city/sights/parks-gardens/bryant-park" class="copy--body__link">Bryant Park</a>, <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/new-york-city/sights/squares-plazas/union-square" class="copy--body__link">Union Square</a>, Columbus Circle and <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/new-york-city/sights/architecture/grand-central-terminal" class="copy--body__link">Grand Central Terminal</a> open at various times throughout the month, as do ice-skating rinks at Bryant Park and <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/new-york-city/sights/architecture/rockefeller-center" class="copy--body__link">Rockefeller Center</a>. Take some time to wander through these warrens of vendor stalls and admire the handiwork on offer, ranging from jewellery and clothing to art, and then sample some of the seasonal culinary delights on display. Each market offers a different setting and allows you to soak up the atmosphere of this city at its most sparkly and alluring.</p><p><em>Rebecca Warren – Destination Editor, Eastern USA. Alternatively, sample hand-rolled <em>Bringoli</em> – a pasta traditionally topped with meatless ragù – at the <a href="http://www.valtiberinaintoscana.it/events/anghiari/festa-di-san-martino-e-dei-bringoli-2016" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">Feast of San Martino</a>.</p><p>Olive harvesting and pressing also starts this month. Get involved at local farms and celebrate at the Olive Oil Festival in Montemurlo (<a href="http://www.festadelloliomontemurlo.it/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">festadelloliomontemurlo.it</a>). But don’t spend all your money on oil – you’ll need to save some for the <a href="http://www.truffleintuscany.it/eng_san_miniato_truffle_in_tuscany/news_truffle_in_tuscany_mostra_mercato_tartufo_san_miniato.html" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">Festival of the White Truffle of San Miniato</a> if you’re a fan of this flavoursome fungus.</p><p>If you’re still hungry, walk through the city walls of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/italy/tuscany/lucca" class="copy--body__link">Lucca</a> to discover the Desco, a large food fair that begins mid-November and continues all month. If you look up from your tasting plate, you might even catch the last of the autumn colours.</p><p><em>Jenny Stephens – blogger at <a href="https://exploreadventurediscover.wordpress.com/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">exploreadventurediscover.wordpress.com</a> and <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/pathfinders" class="copy--body__link">Lonely Planet Pathfinder</a>.</em></p></div>
