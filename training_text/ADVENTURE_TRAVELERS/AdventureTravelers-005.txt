</div><p class="copy--feature">With its grand mountains, rolling hills and stunning coastline, the castle-packed, Welsh-speaking heartland of North Wales has always been an epic place for the active. And&nbsp;the addition of new facilities – including an innovative&nbsp;surf lagoon and record-breaking zip lines – to those age-old charms&nbsp;has created arguably the best place&nbsp;for adventure sports in Britain.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/shutterstock_483362932-1bbb6470fbda.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/mountains-quarries-and-surf-lagoons-adventures-in-north-wales&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/shutterstock_483362932-1bbb6470fbda.jpg&amp;description=A%20hiker%20surveys%20Snowdonia%20from%20Glyder%20Fach%20%C2%A9%20Sebastien%20Coell%20/%20Shutterstock" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107512 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/shutterstock_483362932-1bbb6470fbda.jpg" alt="A hiker surveys Snowdonia from Glyder Fach © Sebastien Coell / Shutterstock"> A hiker surveys Snowdonia from Glyder Fach © Sebastien Coell / Shutterstock</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>Go underground at Llechwedd&nbsp;Slate Caverns</strong></h3><p>Slate mining once dominated the economy in northwest Wales: slate from these hills supplied most of the roofs in Victorian Britain and was transported around the world. Its decline over the last century hit local communities hard and left quarried hillsides, great caverns and dark tunnels in its wake.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Llechwedd-Slate-Caverns-f0f1f1e5994f.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/mountains-quarries-and-surf-lagoons-adventures-in-north-wales&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Llechwedd-Slate-Caverns-f0f1f1e5994f.jpg&amp;description=Trampolines%20and%20zip%20lines%20hang%20in%20cathedral-sized%20caves%20at%20Blaenau%20Ffestiniog%20%C2%A9%20James%20Smart%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107487 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Llechwedd-Slate-Caverns-f0f1f1e5994f.jpg" alt="Trampolines and zip lines hang in cathedral-sized caves at Blaenau Ffestiniog © James Smart / Lonely Planet"> Trampolines and zip lines hang in cathedral-sized caves at Blaenau Ffestiniog © James Smart / Captured on Samsung Galaxy</p><p>They’re all visible around the small town of Blaenau Ffestiniog. Surrounded on all sides by <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/snowdonia-national-park-parc-cenedlaethol-eryri" class="copy--body__link">Snowdonia National Park</a>, its quarry-scarred landscape means it&nbsp;didn't qualify for park status. Yet the area&nbsp;has a hard beauty of its own, and once you head downwards you discover another world. You can explore the <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/a/wales/blaenau-ffestiniog/llechwedd-slate-caverns/pois/sights/1318920/places/360973" class="copy--body__link">Llechwedd Slate Caverns</a> just outside town via numerous tours, and adventurous can spend two hours exploring the eerily magnificent mines on a Caverns tour, using zip lines, rope bridges and footholds hammered into the walls, gazing into dark holes and across cathedral-sized caves. It’s a great feeling – you get to put your hands on history, and set your heart pounding.</p><div class="halfway-links--related media">

Trampolining in a cave is a unique&nbsp;experience, and the atmospheric lighting and chiselled walls around you give the giggling fun of bouncing up and down a nice counterpoint.</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>Fly high and low on record-breaking zip lines</strong></h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Zip-World-Velocity-02a7244db1bb.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/mountains-quarries-and-surf-lagoons-adventures-in-north-wales&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Zip-World-Velocity-02a7244db1bb.jpg&amp;description=Zip%20World%20Velocity%20is%20the%20world" s="" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107519 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Zip-World-Velocity-02a7244db1bb.jpg" alt="Zip World Velocity is the world's fastest zip line © Zip World"> Zip World Velocity is the world's fastest zip line © Zip World</p><p>Outdoor zip lines offer up a different perspective. You can build up quite a lick heading down these, a physical thrill that’s matched by the awesome spectacle of North Wales swooshing by beneath you. Blaenau Ffestiniog’s three zip lines take you down from the hills above to the mine itself. At Bethesda, northwest of Blaenau, Zip World Velocity (<a href="http://www.zipworld.co.uk/location/detail/bethesda" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">zipworld.co.uk</a>) boasts the longest line in Europe and the fastest (up to&nbsp;100mph) in the world.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/photo001-49ee69790966.jpg" class="copy--body__link">Go Below</a>, outside the appealing town of <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/north-wales/betws-y-coed/" class="copy--body__link">Betws-y-Coed</a>, has the world’s longest underground zip line and can take you to the deepest point in Britain that’s accessible to the public – almost 400m below ground.</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>Snowdon: a mountain for all comers</strong></h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/shutterstock_2502564-5ddf6123612e.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a for exploring <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/snowdon-yr-wyddfa" class="copy--body__link">Snowdon</a>, at 1085m the highest point in England and Wales. It’s famous for its views (to Ireland on a – rare – clear day), legends (it’s said to be the tomb of a giant slain by King Arthur) and the fact that you can get a train to its summit. That accessibility is a big part of its appeal, and there are numerous ways to tackle the mountain, from the 120-year-old <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/snowdon-yr-wyddfa/activities/scenic-railway/snowdon-mountain-railway" class="copy--body__link">railway</a>&nbsp;and the zig-zagging Miners' Track to challenging climbing pitches and notorious Crib Goch (a knife-edge arête with a steep slope on one side and a sheer drop on the other).</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>Exploring Snowdonia National Park</strong></h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Tryfan-c14e01947483.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/mountains-quarries-and-surf-lagoons-adventures-in-north-wales&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Tryfan-c14e01947483.jpg&amp;description=Tryfan%20offers%20some%20of%20the%20most%20enjoyable%20scrambling%20in%20Britain%20%C2%A9%20Joe%20Daniel%20Price%20/%20Getty" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-107488 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Tryfan-c14e01947483.jpg" alt="Tryfan offers some of the most enjoyable scrambling in Britain © Joe Daniel Price / Getty"> Tryfan offers some of the most enjoyable scrambling in Britain © Joe Daniel Price / Getty</p><p>Snowdon is the best known – and busiest – feature of Snowdonia National Park, which stretches over 823 sq miles of peaks, valleys, forests and coastline. But there are arguably even better walks to be had in other parts. The Glyders mountain range is a steep mass of broken rocks whose heather- and boulder-covered upper reaches feel like another planet. Tryfan, the most iconic of its peaks, offers invigorating scrambling routes and is topped with two rocks called Adam and Eve – the bold can leap between the two for good luck. Further south, <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/cader-idris-cadair-idris/" class="copy--body__link">Cader Idris</a> is popular with walkers and climbers, while the park as a whole was made a Dark Sky Reserve in 2015 in tribute to its clear air.</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>Mountain biking</strong></h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Coed-Llandegla-533af9ea24a3.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/mountains-quarries-and-surf-lagoons-adventures-in-north-wales&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Coed-Llandegla-533af9ea24a3.jpg&amp;description=Rolling%20through%20woodland%20at%20Coed%20Llandegla%20%C2%A9%20Oneplanet%20Adventure" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-107618 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Coed-Llandegla-533af9ea24a3.jpg" alt="Rolling through woodland at Coed Llandegla © Oneplanet Adventure"> Rolling through woodland at Coed Llandegla © Oneplanet Adventure</p><p>Exploring Wales needn’t just be done in walking boots – the country has some of the world’s best mountain biking facilities. East of the national park, towards Wrexham, Coed Llandegla is a forest reserve with trails operated by Oneplanet (<a href="https://oneplanetadventure.com/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">oneplanetadventure.com</a>) that run the gamut from gentle, family-orientated routes to viciously steep ascents and hardcore jumps and drops. The impressive skills zone is a good place to practice, and the blue trail&nbsp;is great for beginners who want downhill sections that exhilarate without being petrifying.</p><p>Back in the national park, Coed y Brenin (<a href="http://www.beicsbrenin.co.uk/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">beicsbrenin.co.uk</a>), near <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/north-wales/dolgellau" class="copy--body__link">Dolgellau</a>,&nbsp;was the UK’s first dedicated mountain bike centre and now has eight trails including extensive singletrack.</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>Making waves in the valleys</strong></h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/Surf-Snowdonia-50-3b28d7f0748d.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a the landscape,&nbsp;<a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/snowdonia-national-park-parc-cenedlaethol-eryri/activities/theme-parks-attractions/surf-snowdonia" class="copy--body__link">Surf Snowdonia</a>&nbsp;is a ground-breaking surf lagoon that allows you to catch consistent waves in a tree-lined valley miles from the sea. The technology is high-tech and the waves are artificial –&nbsp;they roll along once every 90 seconds, and form predictable heights in different parts of the lagoon, making them great for learning on – but the setting in a wide valley outside Dolgarrog is appealing and natural enough to make the experience&nbsp;thoroughly satisfying. Eco-pod accommodation, other activities including surf kayaking and a comedic ‘blob’ that launches participants high in the air complete the picture. If you’re inspired to get a taste of local&nbsp;seawater,&nbsp;try&nbsp;<a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/rhosneigr" class="copy--body__link">Rhosneigr</a> or <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/aberdaron" class="copy--body__link">Aberdaron</a>.</p><h3 class="copy--h3"><strong>The best of the rest</strong></h3><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/GettyRF_593129203_2-27c0dfdb7dee.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/mountains-quarries-and-surf-lagoons-adventures-in-north-wales&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/GettyRF_593129203_2-27c0dfdb7dee.jpg&amp;description=A%20lighthouse%20looks%20from%20Anglesey%20to%20the%20Welsh%20mainland%20%C2%A9%20Ray%20Wise%20/%20Getty%20Images" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-107532 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/10/GettyRF_593129203_2-27c0dfdb7dee.jpg" alt="A lighthouse looks from Anglesey to the Welsh mainland © Ray Wise / Getty Images"> A lighthouse looks from Anglesey to the Welsh mainland © Ray Wise / Getty Images</p><p>The range of landscapes and ingenuity of local tour guides mean there are countless other activities to undertake in North Wales. The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/north-wales/isle-of-anglesey" class="copy--body__link">Isle of Anglesey</a>&nbsp;has druidic history&nbsp;and offers coasteering, windsurfing and puffin-spotting trips,&nbsp;rafting can be undertaken at various locations including <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/north-wales/bala-y-bala/activities/water-sports/national-white-water-centre" class="copy--body__link">Bala</a>&nbsp;and hikers&nbsp;can get away from Snowdon's from the Quay hotel in Deganwy © James Smart / Lonely Planet"> Conwy, with its distinctive castle, seen from the Quay hotel in Deganwy © James Smart /&nbsp;Captured on Samsung Galaxy</p><p>There are good bases across northwest Wales, with Betws-y-Coed and <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/wales/north-wales/llanberis" class="copy--body__link">Llanberis</a> (both near Snowdon), <a While trains and buses connect the key towns, wider exploration&nbsp;is easiest with your own vehicle. Tour companies across the region can also help arrange trips.</p><p><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">James&nbsp;travelled to&nbsp;North Wales&nbsp;with support from VisitWales.
