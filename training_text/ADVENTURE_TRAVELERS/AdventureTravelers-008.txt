</div><p class="copy--feature"><span class="feature">The secret of Montenegro’s gorgeous coastline is well and truly out; meanwhile, those in the know have been turning their compasses towards its epic northern highlands. The spirit of adventure steers travellers off the trodden path in this corner of the Balkans, where boundless hospitality and old-world customs rule the rugged landscape. No trip to Montenegro is complete without experiencing the highlights of the north – after all, its name doesn’t mean ‘Black Mountain’ for nothing.</span></p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Grbaja-valley-prokletije-Montenegro_CS-4fa30db9aede.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Grbaja-valley-prokletije-Montenegro_CS-4fa30db9aede.jpg&amp;description=Grbaja%20Valley%20and%20Prokletije%20Mountains,%20on%20the%20Peaks%20of%20the%20Balkans%20trail%20%C2%A9%20Jovan%20Nikolic%20/%20Getty%20Images" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-106697 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Grbaja-valley-prokletije-Montenegro_CS-4fa30db9aede.jpg" alt="Grbaja Valley and Prokletije Mountains, on the Peaks of the Balkans trail © Jovan Nikolic / Getty Images"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Grbaja Valley and Prokletije Mountains, on the Peaks of the Balkans trail © Jovan Nikolic / Getty Images</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Hike the Peaks of the Balkans</h3><p>The majestic wilderness of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/prokletije-mountains" class="copy--body__link">Prokletije</a> (aka the Accursed Mountains) is home to Montenegro’s newest national park and the cross-border Peaks of the Balkans (<a href="http://www.peaksofthebalkans.com/index.php?lang=en" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">peaksofthebalkans.com</a>) hiking trail. Here you can have one of Europe’s most remote corners and incredible scenery almost to yourself. This 192km circuit follows shepherds’ paths and alpine trails – soaring over 2000m – through forests, meadows and isolated villages of Montenegro, Albania and Kosovo. Not up for the 10-day, three-country loop? A 27km hike within Montenegro from <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/plav" class="copy--body__link">Plav</a> (the gateway for&nbsp;the trail) to the village of Vusanje is an equally marvellous&nbsp;alternative&nbsp;offering&nbsp;fantastic views of the jagged Karanfil Mountains.</p><p>Going with a guide is recommended. If you aren’t joining an organised trek, contact Plav’s tourist office (<a href="http://www.toplav.me/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">toplav.me</a>) to sort out the border-crossing requirements and accommodation, which includes welcoming village homestays, mountain huts and ecolodges spread along the trail.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/rafting-Tara-canyon-Montenegro-520f8e74b6f1.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/rafting-Tara-canyon-Montenegro-520f8e74b6f1.jpg&amp;description=Rafting%20in%20the%20Tara%20canyon%20%C2%A9%20courtesy%20of%20Montenegro%20National%20Tourism%20Organisation" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-106679 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/rafting-Tara-canyon-Montenegro-520f8e74b6f1.jpg" alt="Rafting in the Tara canyon © courtesy of Montenegro National Tourism Organisation"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Rafting in the Tara canyon © courtesy of Montenegro National Tourism Organisation</span></p><div class="halfway-links--related media">

</div><h3 class="copy--h3">Raft the Tara river</h3><p>For a different perspective on&nbsp;the highland scenery, rafting the&nbsp;emerald-green&nbsp;<a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/northern-montenegro/durmitor-national-park/sights/natural-landmarks/tara-canyon" class="copy--body__link">Tara Canyon</a>&nbsp;(at 1300m, one of the world’s deepest) beneath the soaring Durmitor peaks can’t be beaten. Relatively easy rapids through most of the season (except for April and May, thanks to the melting snow) make it a suitable experience even for white-water novices. A two-day trip takes in the 82km stretch through the deepest part of the canyon, from the stunning&nbsp;<a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/northern-montenegro/durmitor-national-park/sights/architecture/tara-bridge" class="copy--body__link">Tara Bridge</a> to Šćepan Polje on the Bosnian border; day trips cover the final 18km of the route, where&nbsp;most of the rapids are found.</p><p>Camp Grab (<a href="http://www.tara-grab.com/?lang=en" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">tara-grab.com</a>), secluded in the canyon 9km upstream from Šćepan Polje, is a recommended operator for multi-day rafting. Lodgings include tents, bungalows and en-suite rooms, and the mouth-watering lunches make it a complete package. For day trips, the experienced Kljajević Luka camp (<a href="http://tara-rafting.info/?lang=en" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">tara-rafting.info</a>) is conveniently located near the Tara bridge.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/mountain-biking-Durmitor-national-park-Montenegro-641266493d13.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/mountain-biking-Durmitor-national-park-Montenegro-641266493d13.jpg&amp;description=Mountain%20biking%20on%20the%20Durmitor%20Ring%20%C2%A9%20courtesy%20of%20Montenegro%20National%20Tourism%20Organisation" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-106678 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/mountain-biking-Durmitor-national-park-Montenegro-641266493d13.jpg" alt="Mountain biking on the Durmitor Ring © courtesy of Montenegro National Tourism Organisation"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Mountain biking on the Durmitor Ring © courtesy of Montenegro National Tourism Organisation</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Cycle the Durmitor Ring</h3><p>The glorious <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/northern-montenegro/durmitor-national-park" class="copy--body__link">Durmitor National Park</a>, with its dramatic&nbsp;canyons, glacial lakes (poetically nicknamed ‘mountain eyes’) and hulking&nbsp;limestone peaks, is prime mountain biking territory. An 85km paved road – known as the Durmitor Ring – winds through this splendid scenery, across vast pastures and past several vertigo-inducing viewpoints above Tara and Sušica canyons. With some fairly steep ascents, the loop demands suitable fitness; it starts and ends at Žabljak town, while villages along the way have good restaurants or cafes where you can break the ride.</p><p>Family-run <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/northern-montenegro/durmitor-national-park/hotels/etno-selo-sljeme" class="copy--body__link">Etno Selo Šljeme</a> near Žabljak is a terrific&nbsp;Durmitor stay offering snug two-bedroom cabins with pristine mountain views. Its&nbsp;excellent restaurant serves traditional dishes; highland specialty <em>jagnjetina ispod sača</em> (lamb roasted under hot coals) is a must-try.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Hrid-lake-Prokletije-Montenegro_CS-c827478e90e7.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Hrid-lake-Prokletije-Montenegro_CS-c827478e90e7.jpg&amp;description=Hrid%20lake%20in%20Prokletije%20National%20Park%20%C2%A9%20Lenar%20Musin%20/%20Getty%20Images" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="size-full wp-image-106698 stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Hrid-lake-Prokletije-Montenegro_CS-c827478e90e7.jpg" alt="Hrid lake in Prokletije National Park © Lenar Musin / Getty Images"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Hrid lake in Prokletije National Park © Lenar Musin / Getty Images</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Look for fairies at Hrid lake</h3><p>Legend has it that fairies visit Hrid lake – at 1970m, it’s Montenegro’s highest – to bathe at night. Once you catch a glimpse of this blissfully quiet, dark-green beauty of Prokletije, you may be tempted to join them and never leave. A 6.8km hike through meadows and pine forest from Eco resort Hrid (<a href="http://www.toplav.me/home/smjestaj/?lang=en" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">toplav.me</a>) at Babino Polje, with gurgling streams of cool water and wild berries to pick along the way, brings you to the lake. Its mystical appeal inspires locals to swim here for good health; if you’d rather not brave the mountain chill, throw a customary coin into the water and make a wish before you leave.</p><p>Samel’s Cottage (samelovakolibahrid@gmail.com), near Bajrovića <em>katun</em> (mountain huts), is the perfect base for Hrid lake. Its&nbsp;gazebo views over the Accursed Mountains are hard to forget, especially when paired with homemade treats like delicious <em>urmašice</em> (golden biscuits soaked in syrup).</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Dalovica-cave-Montenegro-2b1e2309b1f6.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Dalovica-cave-Montenegro-2b1e2309b1f6.jpg&amp;description=The%20huge%20%C6%89alovi%C4%87a%20cave%20in%20northern%20Montenegro%20%C2%A9%20courtesy%20of%20Rams%20Travel%20/%20Stefan%20Vuki%C4%87evi%C4%87" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-106747 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Dalovica-cave-Montenegro-2b1e2309b1f6.jpg" alt="The huge Ɖalovića cave in northern Montenegro © courtesy of Rams Travel / Stefan Vukićević"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">The huge Ɖalovića cave in northern Montenegro © courtesy of Rams Travel / Stefan Vukićević</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Descend into massive Ɖalovića cave</h3><p>If you get tired of scaling Montenegro’s peaks, hidden in the mountains of the north (about 40km from <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/bijelo-polje" class="copy--body__link">Bijelo Polje</a>) is the Ɖalovića cave, the country’s longest: only 17.5km of its impressive&nbsp;galleries and passages have been explored so far. Reaching the cave is an adventure in itself: a 3.7km path leads through bucolic Ɖalovića gorge (formed by the Bistrica river), past smaller caves, springs and a waterfall,&nbsp;to&nbsp;the cave’s entrance above the so-called ‘Devil’s Whirlpools’. The trek begins at the 17th-century Podvrh monastery, whose modest exterior hides precious ancient frescoes and a gold-plated iconostasis. While the cave is not yet open for tourists, local guides with proper equipment can take small groups inside.</p><p>The Green Valley household (<a href="http://www.northernexposure.me/365/en/rural-tourism/bistrica/rural-household-green-valley/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">northernexposure.me</a>) in Bistrica village near the Ɖalovića gorge provides comfortable rooms for travellers and tasty home-cooked meals such as peppers baked with cheese. If you’d rather trot than trek, the hosts can organise horse riding to the gorge.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Ethno-village-Montenegro-cheese-museum-a6cf1521fb92.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Ethno-village-Montenegro-cheese-museum-a6cf1521fb92.jpg&amp;description=Museum%20of%20Cheese%20at%20Etno%20Selo%20Montenegro%20%C2%A9%20Brana%20Vladisavljevic%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-106826 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Ethno-village-Montenegro-cheese-museum-a6cf1521fb92.jpg" alt="Museum of Cheese at Etno Selo Montenegro © Brana Vladisavljevic / Lonely Planet"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Museum of Cheese at Etno Selo Montenegro © Brana Vladisavljevic / Lonely Planet</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Visit Montenegro’s first ethno village</h3><p>The quirky Etno Selo Montenegro (<a href="http://etnoselo.me/?lang=en" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">etnoselo.me</a>), 34km from <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/niksic" class="copy--body__link">Nikšić</a>, was the first ‘ethno village’ in the country. Besides cute wooden and stone cottages, it has plenty of character with rooms dedicated to comics, wool, cheese, ’70s and ’80s Balkan memorabilia and a Monty Python-esque ‘museum of Montenegrin air’. The rustic, homely restaurant serves 15 types of <em>rakija</em> (fruit brandy), a range of mountain teas, homemade juices and local wines, while the menu features Montenegrin delicacies&nbsp;like the famous Njeguški cheese and <em>pršut</em> (smoke-dried ham).</p><p>In addition to a Russian <em>banya</em> (spa), an outdoor pool and kids’ playground, there&nbsp;are&nbsp;200km of walking trails to explore nearby. The friendly staff can organise anything from rafting, biking and horse riding to jeep safaris&nbsp;and&nbsp;cruises at <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/northern-montenegro/sights/natural-landmarks/piva-canyon" class="copy--body__link">Piva lake</a>. Less active types can try breaking the current record (36 hours) at the hilarious lying-down competition, held here every August.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Eko-katun-Vranjak-Montenegro-7ec645f78b83.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-tips-and-articles/top-experiences-in-montenegros-northern-highlands&amp;media=http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Eko-katun-Vranjak-Montenegro-7ec645f78b83.jpg&amp;description=Eko%20Katun%20Vranjak%20on%20Bjelasica%20mountain%20%C2%A9%20Brana%20Vladisavljevic%20/%20Lonely%20Planet" class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-106671 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" src="//images-resrc.staticlp.com/S=W748/O=75/http://www.lonelyplanet.com/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Eko-katun-Vranjak-Montenegro-7ec645f78b83.jpg" alt="Eko Katun Vranjak on Bjelasica mountain © Brana Vladisavljevic / Lonely Planet"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">Eko Katun Vranjak on Bjelasica mountain © Brana Vladisavljevic / Lonely Planet</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Call it a night in a <em>katun</em>
</h3><p>You haven’t really experienced Montenegro’s highlands if you didn’t spend a chilly night under the stars in a <em>katun</em>. Originally shepherds’ huts in summer pastures, <em>katuns</em>&nbsp;are now authentic lodgings for travellers dotted across the region.&nbsp; At an altitude of 1780m, Eco Katun Vranjak (<a href="http://jelka.me/welcome/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">jelka.me</a>) on Bjelasica mountain was the first such ‘eco village’ in Montenegro. The cozy wooden cabins with up to seven beds and two-person huts come with solar power, shared showers and open-fire common area. The top-notch kitchen serves local fare made from home-grown ingredients; try warm <em>priganice</em> (fritters) with cheese, honey and jam for a sublime fresh-air breakfast.</p><p>Classic&nbsp;<em>katun&nbsp;</em>adventures include&nbsp;hiking, mountain biking or horse riding across the serene, windswept slopes of Bjelasica – the only mountain of volcanic origin in Montenegro – to Crna Glava peak (2139m) and <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/biogradska-gora-national-park" class="copy--body__link">Biogradska Gora National Park</a>, one of Europe’s last remaining primeval forests.</p><p class="stack__article__image-container"><a href="/travel-blog/tip-article/wordpress_uploads/2016/09/Montenegro-Savardak-restaurant-d1f2c3ac9eee.jpg" class="copy--body__link"></a><a class="social__item social__item--pinterest social__item--pinned" target="blank">  <span class="accessibility">Pin this image</span>  <span class="social__icon icon--pinterest icon--white"></span></a><img class="wp-image-106673 size-full stack__article__image is-landscape" Savardak restaurant near Kolašin © Brana Vladisavljevic / Lonely Planet"> <span class="copy--caption img--caption">The haystack-shaped Savardak restaurant near Kolašin © Brana Vladisavljevic / Lonely Planet</span></p><h3 class="copy--h3">Feast on&nbsp;slow food <em>savardak</em>-style</h3><p>While original <em>savardaks</em> – homes once typical of Montenegro’s highlands – may be long gone, <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/kolasin/restaurants/european/savardak" class="copy--body__link">Savardak</a> restaurant on the outskirts of <a href="https://www.lonelyplanet.com/montenegro/kolasin" class="copy--body__link">Kolašin</a> is not to be missed if travelling in these parts today. Painstakingly built from straw and wood as a&nbsp;haystack-shaped hut and lovingly decorated with authentic tools from the region, its&nbsp;interior is truly atmospheric.&nbsp;But the real reason to visit is the Kolašin <em>kačamak</em>, a simple dish cooked with wheat flour, layered handmade mountain cheese, <em>kajmak</em> (salty clotted cream) and potatoes; just as creamy and cheesy, <em>cicvara</em> is similar but made with cornflour and without potatoes. Gulp down&nbsp;all this goodness&nbsp;with dollops of refreshing <em>kiselo mlijeko</em> (sour cream). Savardak is a fine example of Montenegro’s emerging slow-food movement, focusing on traditional cooking methods and organic&nbsp;local ingredients.</p><p>Sampling Montenegrin food&nbsp;is incomplete without&nbsp;<em>rakija</em>, the stutteringly strong fruit brandy usually&nbsp;distilled&nbsp;at home; more than just a welcome drink, it’s a universal cure for ailments and part of any meal. For some apple, pear or plum <em>rakija</em>-tasting in rustic surroundings, head to Balšić household in the hills around Bijelo Polje – contact the tourist office (<a href="http://www.tobijelopolje.me" class="copy--body__link">tobijelopolje.me</a>) for information. Time your visit for autumn to observe the old-style method of <em>rakija </em>‘baking’ in a burning cauldron.</p><p><em>Brana travelled to northern&nbsp;Montenegro&nbsp;with support from Rams Travel&nbsp;(<a href="http://www.ramstravel.co.me/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">ramstravel.co.me</a>) and the Regional development agency for Bjelasica, Komovi and Prokletije (<a href="http://www.bjelasica-komovi.me/en/" target="_blank" class="copy--body__link">bjelasica-komovi.me</a>).
